# klove Brand Guidelines

klove is an AI-native platform that makes websites interactive and readable for
AI agents. Early-stage B2B SaaS targeting Marketing/Growth teams and
CTOs/Engineers. The brand is forward-thinking, bold, a little edgy — but always
articulate and grounded.

## Brand Identity

### What klove does
klove makes websites interactive and readable for AI agents. The web was built
for humans. klove rebuilds it for the agents running on top of them.

### Positioning statement (internal use)
klove sits at the intersection of web infrastructure and the agentic AI wave.
It's a platform play — the pick-and-shovel for teams who want their web
properties to work with AI, not against it.

### What this means in practice for copy
- The problem klove solves is real and urgent — AI agents are already crawling
  the web and most websites are invisible or broken to them
- The audience already feels this pain; klove doesn't need to educate, it needs
  to validate and then solve
- Copy should feel like it was written by someone building in this space —
  not a marketer describing it from the outside

## Voice and Tone

### The klove voice is:

**Forward-thinking** — klove sees where things are going before others do. Copy
should feel like it comes from inside the wave, not outside looking in. Write
as if the AI-native web is already here and klove is the infrastructure for it.

**Bold** — Direct. No hedging. No "may help you potentially consider..."
Say the thing. Own the claim. If it's true, say it plainly.

**A little edgy** — Not aggressive. Not crude. More like: willing to say the
uncomfortable thing that everyone in the room is already thinking. A raised
eyebrow where others would say nothing.

**Articulate** — The boldness is earned by precision. klove's audience
(engineers and growth leads) respects concision and punishes fluff. Every word
should mean something.

### The klove voice is NOT:

- Corporate ("leverage synergies to unlock value")
- AI-generated-sounding (see: Hard Rules below)
- Vague ("klove helps businesses grow")
- Over-hyped ("game-changing," "revolutionary," "disruptive")
- Overly casual or self-deprecating
- Preachy or lecture-y

### Tone calibration by platform

| Platform     | Tone dial                                              |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Website      | Confident + precise. Outcomes first. Show don't tell. |
| LinkedIn     | Articulate + a little provocative. Short paragraphs.  |
| Twitter/X    | Sharp. One point. No fluff. Can have attitude.         |
| Instagram    | Visual-led. Short punchy lines. Brand-forward.         |
| TikTok       | Conversational. Informal but not sloppy. Fast-paced.  |

## Hard Rules — What to Always Avoid

These are non-negotiable across all platforms and content types:

1. **No em dashes** (—) in copy. Use a comma, a period, or restructure the
   sentence. Em dashes read as AI-generated.

2. **No jargon for jargon's sake.** If a CTG or growth lead has to pause to
   decode a word, rewrite it.

3. **No filler superlatives.** Avoid: "powerful," "robust," "seamless,"
   "cutting-edge," "next-generation," "world-class."

4. **No passive voice.** If you wrote "results are delivered," rewrite it to
   "klove delivers results."

5. **No generic AI copy patterns.** Avoid: starting sentences with "In today's
   fast-paced world," or "In conclusion," or stacking three-word lists at the
   end of every paragraph.

6. **No corporate hedging.** "May," "might," "could potentially" — remove them.
   Say what klove does. Say it once. Say it cleanly.

7. **Never capitalise "klove"** — it's always lowercase, even at the start of a
   sentence. Restructure the sentence if needed to avoid sentence-start
   capitalisation.

## Messaging Framework

### Primary message (website / top-of-funnel)
The web wasn't built for AI agents. klove makes it work like it was.

### Message by audience segment

**For CTOs / Engineers:**
- klove is infrastructure, not a plugin
- Speak to reliability, integration depth, and control
- They care about how it works and whether it will break production
- Lead with: architecture, compatibility, API design, agent protocols

**For Marketing / Growth:**
- klove is a distribution unlock
- Speak to reach, discoverability, and pipeline impact
- They care about whether AI-driven traffic converts and whether it's attributable
- Lead with: AI search visibility, agent-driven conversions, content reach

### Proof points to reference (when available)
- Early customer use cases
- Agent protocols supported
- Integrations live
- Time-to-value

## Copy Standards

For full frameworks (AIDA, PAS, FAB, headline formulas, CTAs, objection
handling), see: `references/copy-frameworks.md`

### Core rules applied to klove copy

**Lead with outcomes, not process.**
Bad: "klove parses your site structure and exposes it to agent APIs."
Good: "klove makes your website visible to every AI agent searching the web."

**Be specific.**
Bad: "klove helps teams move faster."
Good: "klove cuts agent onboarding time from days to minutes."

**Speak to one person.**
Write to a CTO at a 30-person SaaS, or a Head of Growth at a Series A startup.
Not "businesses" or "organizations" or "teams."

**Earn the claim before making it.**
Don't open with "klove is the leading platform for..." — earn that statement
with a concrete proof point first.

## Platform-Specific Guidance

For detailed platform formats, character limits, and post structures, see:
`references/platform-guides.md`

### Summary

**LinkedIn:** 3-5 short paragraphs. Hook in line 1 (before "see more"). One
clear point per post. End with a question or statement, not a hashtag dump.

**Twitter/X:** One idea. Under 240 characters where possible. Threads only if
the idea needs it. Never start with "Just shipped..." unless it's genuinely big.

**Website:** Outcomes above the fold. Features only after desire is established.
Single CTA per section. No carousels of benefits — pick the one that matters.

**Instagram:** Visuals carry the message. Copy is support, not the story.
Bold short lines. Brand palette visible.

**TikTok/Video:** Hook in the first 2 seconds. Script out loud before writing.
If it sounds weird spoken, rewrite it.
