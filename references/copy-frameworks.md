## Core Principle

Every piece of klove copy must do four things:
1. **Grab attention** — stop the scroll or the skip
2. **Create desire** — make them feel what klove unlocks
3. **Remove friction** — address the doubts before they form
4. **Prompt action** — one clear next step

If it fails at any one, rewrite it.

## Framework 1: AIDA

Use for: landing pages, email campaigns, LinkedIn posts, product pages.

```
ATTENTION:  Bold headline — the promise or the pain
INTEREST:   Elaborate on the problem (make them nod "yes, that's me")
DESIRE:     Show the transformation — what changes when klove is in place
ACTION:     Single clear CTA
```

### klove AIDA Example (Landing page)

```
ATTENTION:
"Your website is invisible to AI agents. klove fixes that."

INTEREST:
"AI agents are crawling the web right now — searching, querying, making
decisions. Most websites can't respond to them. That means missed traffic,
missed conversions, and a growing gap between you and the teams who built
for this."

DESIRE:
"klove makes your site readable, queryable, and interactive to any AI agent.
Your content shows up in AI search. Your flows work with agent-driven input.
You stop losing ground to teams who moved earlier."

ACTION:
"Book a 20-minute walkthrough"
```

## Framework 2: PAS

Use for: pain-driven top-of-funnel, cold outreach, email subject lines,
LinkedIn hooks.

```
PROBLEM:   State the problem plainly
AGITATE:   Make the cost of inaction feel real
SOLUTION:  klove as the fix — concise, specific
```

### klove PAS Example (Cold email)

```
PROBLEM:
"AI agents can't read most websites. Including probably yours."

AGITATE:
"As AI-driven search and agent-based discovery scales, sites that aren't
readable to agents stop showing up. You don't lose traffic all at once.
You just slowly stop being found."

SOLUTION:
"klove makes your site agent-readable in hours. Not a plugin. Not a
workaround. Infrastructure that works with your existing stack."
```

## Framework 3: FAB

Use for: product pages, feature announcements, sales decks, comparison content.

```
FEATURE:    What it does (the fact)
ADVANTAGE:  Why that matters vs. alternatives
BENEFIT:    What the customer actually gets
```

### klove FAB Example

```
FEATURE:
"klove exposes your site's structure and content through a standardised
agent API."

ADVANTAGE:
"Unlike schema markup or basic SEO, klove doesn't just describe your site
to search engines — it makes it fully queryable and interactive for AI agents."

BENEFIT:
"Your content gets cited in AI responses. Your flows work in agent-driven
contexts. You're not playing catch-up when agent traffic becomes the norm."
```

## Headlines That Hook

The headline decides whether the rest gets read. Optimise it first.

| Formula            | klove Example                                             |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| The Problem        | "Your website doesn't exist to AI agents. Yet."           |
| The Promise        | "Make your site readable to every AI agent on the web."   |
| The How-To         | "How to make your website work with AI agents, not against them" |
| The Number         | "3 reasons your site is invisible to AI-driven search."   |
| The Curiosity Gap  | "The infrastructure gap most SaaS teams haven't noticed." |
| The Transformation | "From invisible to indexed — how klove changes your AI surface area" |
| The Direct         | "The web changed. klove helps your site keep up."         |

### Headline rules for klove
- Lead with the outcome or the problem — not the product name
- Be specific. "AI agents" is better than "AI." "Agent-readable" is better than "optimised."
- "klove" should rarely be in the headline — earn the brand mention first
- Avoid question headlines that have an obvious "no" answer ("Want more traffic?" — weak)

## CTAs That Convert

### CTA formula
[Action Verb] + [What They Get] + [Friction Removal or Urgency]

### klove CTA examples by goal

| Goal               | CTA                                             |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Demo request       | "Book a 20-minute walkthrough"                  |
| Free trial / signup| "Get klove on your site today"                  |
| Content / resource | "See how agent-readiness works"                 |
| Waitlist           | "Join the early access list"                    |
| Case study         | "Read how [Company] made it work"               |

### CTA placement
- Above the fold — before they scroll, give them somewhere to go
- After explaining value — never before you've earned it
- After social proof — a testimonial followed by a CTA converts better than either alone
- Never more than one CTA per section

## Emotional Triggers

Use emotion to hook. Use logic to justify. Don't flip the order.

| Trigger             | When to use it for klove                          | Example                                              |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Fear of falling behind | When audience is aware AI is reshaping search | "Teams that got agent-ready in 2024 are already ahead." |
| Fear of invisibility | Top-of-funnel, awareness content               | "If AI agents can't read your site, you're already losing traffic you don't know about." |
| Desire for edge     | CTOs, growth leads who want to move first       | "Most of your competitors haven't figured this out yet." |
| Desire for simplicity | Engineers who expect infra to be painful      | "klove works with your stack. Nothing to rip out."   |
| Validation          | For teams already feeling this problem         | "Yes, this is a real gap. Yes, it's growing."        |

## Handling Objections

Address doubts before they form. Build these into landing pages, FAQs, and
email sequences.

| Objection                           | klove response                                              |
| ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Is this actually a problem yet?"   | "AI-driven traffic is already measurable. It's not coming — it's here." |
| "We already have SEO covered."      | "SEO optimises for search engine crawlers. klove is for AI agents — different protocol, different output." |
| "This sounds complex to implement." | "klove integrates with your existing site. Most teams are live in under a day." |
| "We're too early for this."         | "The teams treating this as infrastructure now are the ones who won't have to scramble later." |
| "What if agent traffic doesn't matter for us?" | "Run the audit first. The data usually surprises people." |

### Where to place objection-handling copy
- FAQ sections (explicit and searchable)
- Beneath the main CTA (handle the last doubt before they leave)
- In testimonials (let customers answer the objection)

## Build Trust with Proof

Claims need proof to land. Types of proof for klove at early stage:

1. **Customer quotes** — specific outcomes, not vague praise. Include name, role, company.
2. **Use case specifics** — "X team integrated klove and [result] in [timeframe]"
3. **Protocol/integration data** — "klove supports [X] agent protocols" is credible to engineers
4. **Founding team credibility** — if relevant, lean on domain experience
5. **Early access social proof** — "Joined by [X] teams in beta" signals momentum

### Placement
Intersperse proof throughout the page. Don't dump it in a single testimonials
section — put it near the relevant claim.

## Writing Checklist

Before submitting any copy, confirm:

- [ ] "klove" is lowercase throughout
- [ ] No em dashes used
- [ ] Lead with outcome, not process
- [ ] Single CTA per section
- [ ] No filler superlatives (powerful, seamless, robust)
- [ ] No passive voice
- [ ] Specific over generic
- [ ] Reads like a human wrote it — say it out loud
