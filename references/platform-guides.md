## LinkedIn

### Audience on this platform
CTOs, VPs of Engineering, Heads of Growth, Marketing Directors, founders.
They are busy and scroll fast. They reward insight and punish performance.

### Format principles
- **Line 1 is everything.** It's all they see before "see more." Make it earn the click.
- Short paragraphs — 1 to 2 lines max. White space is not wasted space.
- One idea per post. Don't try to cover three things.
- End with a statement or an open question — not a hashtag wall.
- 3 to 5 hashtags maximum, placed at the end, not inline.

### Post structures that work for klove

**Structure 1: Observation + Tension + klove angle**
```
[Observation about the AI/web landscape — 1 line]

[The tension or problem this creates — 2-3 lines]

[What klove does about it — 1-2 lines]

[Question or point that invites response]
```

**Structure 2: Counterintuitive take**
```
[Statement that challenges a common assumption — 1 line]

[Explain why the assumption is wrong — 3-4 lines]

[What's actually true — 2 lines]

[Implication for the reader]
```

**Structure 3: Short story + lesson**
```
[Specific scenario a CTO or growth lead would recognise — 2-3 lines]

[What usually happens — 1-2 lines]

[What klove changes — 1-2 lines]
```

### Character / format limits
- Optimal length: 150 to 300 words
- Hook (before "see more"): under 210 characters
- Max post length: 3,000 characters
- Image: 1200 x 627px recommended
- Video: 256MB max, 3 seconds to 10 minutes

### What not to do on LinkedIn
- Don't start with "Excited to share..." or "Thrilled to announce..."
- Don't use "I" as the first word
- Don't stack bullets in the body — this reads as a listicle, not a post
- Don't end with "Like and share if you agree"
- Don't use more than 5 hashtags

## Twitter / X

### Audience on this platform
Technical founders, early-adopter engineers, growth practitioners, AI builders.
They are skeptical and fast. Wit and specificity win. Vagueness gets ignored.

### Format principles
- One idea, fully expressed. If it needs a thread, it needs a thread — but
  earn it with the first tweet.
- Threads: hook tweet should stand alone. Number your tweets (1/ format).
- Short sentences. Active voice. No wasted words.
- Attitude is allowed. Hedging is not.

### Tweet structures that work for klove

**Single tweet — Sharp take**
```
[Claim or observation — one sentence]

[The reason or implication — one or two sentences]

[Optional: one-line kicker]
```

**Thread — Educational or breakdown**
```
Tweet 1 (hook): Bold claim or counterintuitive statement
Tweet 2-n:      One proof point or step per tweet
Final tweet:    Summary or CTA (e.g. "klove handles this. Link in bio.")
```

### Character limits
- Single tweet: 280 characters
- Aim for under 240 to avoid truncation in some views
- Images: 1200 x 675px
- Video: up to 2 minutes 20 seconds (standard), 60 minutes (Premium)

### What not to do on Twitter / X
- Don't start a tweet with "Hot take:" — it signals low-quality takes
- Don't add "RT if you agree" — it reads as engagement farming
- Don't use more than 2 hashtags per tweet
- Don't post threads longer than 8-10 tweets without very strong content

## Website / Blog

### Audience on this platform
Mixed — engineers evaluating the product, growth leads researching solutions,
founders Googling the problem. Intent varies from awareness to purchase.

### Homepage / Landing page principles

**Above the fold:**
- Headline: outcome or problem — not product description
- Subhead: one sentence that clarifies who it's for and what changes
- Single CTA: clear action, friction-minimised
- No more than 80 words above the fold

**Page structure:**
```
1. Hero — problem or outcome. Single CTA.
2. Social proof — early logos, quote, or stat
3. Problem section — articulate the pain in their language
4. Solution section — what klove does, shown not just told
5. Feature details — for the engineers evaluating
6. More social proof — use case or testimonial
7. CTA section — repeat the primary CTA with friction removal
```

**Body copy rules:**
- Paragraphs: 2-3 sentences max
- Section headers: outcome-led ("What changes when klove is live"),
  not feature-led ("Platform features")
- No hero carousels — they kill conversion
- One CTA per section, same destination across the page

### Blog post structure
```
Headline:     Specific, outcome- or curiosity-driven
Intro:        Hook + context + what you'll learn (under 100 words)
Body:         One point per section. Headers that could stand alone.
Conclusion:   Summary + one actionable takeaway
CTA:          Contextual — doesn't have to be "sign up." Could be a related post.
```

### SEO considerations for agent-era content
- Target queries AI agents and AI search surfaces are likely to answer
- Use structured content (headers, lists, definitions) — these are easier for
  agents to parse (fitting, for klove)
- Include concrete specifics: numbers, product names, protocols

## Instagram

### Audience on this platform
Broader awareness play — founders, operators, AI-curious professionals.
More casual browsing context than LinkedIn or Twitter.

### Format principles
- The visual does the heavy lifting. Copy is support.
- Caption: hook in line 1 (before "more"). Under 125 characters.
- Keep body copy under 150 words for most posts.
- Use line breaks generously — walls of text don't work here.
- 5-10 hashtags, placed at end or in first comment.

### Post types for klove

**Product / feature announcement:**
```
[Visual: product screenshot or branded graphic]
Caption: Short bold line. One sentence on what it means. CTA ("link in bio")
```

**Thought leadership / perspective:**
```
[Visual: bold text graphic with klove brand palette]
Caption: 2-3 short lines. Statement or question.
```

**Behind the scenes / team:**
```
[Visual: real team photo or product in action]
Caption: Casual tone. 1-2 sentences. Human.
```

### Image specs
- Feed post: 1080 x 1080px (square) or 1080 x 1350px (portrait)
- Story: 1080 x 1920px
- Reel cover: 1080 x 1920px

### What not to do on Instagram
- Don't paste a LinkedIn post into the caption — they're different formats
- Don't use klove's colour palette as the only visual — add texture and photography
- Don't use more than 10 hashtags

## TikTok

### Audience on this platform
Younger operators, AI-curious builders, viral discovery — this is brand
awareness and culture-building, not direct conversion.

### Format principles
- Hook in the first 2 seconds — visual or spoken
- 15 to 60 seconds is the sweet spot for reach
- Script it out loud before writing. If it sounds weird spoken, rewrite it.
- Subtitles on — most people watch muted
- Informal is fine. Sloppy is not.

### Content formats for klove

**"Did you know" / fast fact:**
```
Hook: "AI agents can't read most websites. Here's why that matters."
Body: Quick explanation in plain language. 3-4 points.
End:  "klove fixes this. Link in bio."
```

**Hot take / opinion:**
```
Hook: "Unpopular opinion: SEO is not the same as agent-readiness."
Body: Make the case in 30-40 seconds. Direct. Confident.
End:  Statement or open question.
```

**Behind the product:**
```
Hook: "Here's what klove actually does under the hood."
Body: Simple walkthrough. Not a product demo — a story.
End:  "Want to see it on your site? Link in bio."
```

### Video specs
- Aspect ratio: 9:16 (vertical)
- Resolution: 1080 x 1920px
- Max length: 10 minutes (aim for under 60 seconds)
- File size: under 287.6MB

### What not to do on TikTok
- Don't repurpose a LinkedIn post as a script — it will sound stiff
- Don't add subtitles as an afterthought — build them into the edit
- Don't use trending sounds that don't fit the brand tone
